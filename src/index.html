How to Use Blackbox Lab

Five minutes from log to understanding.

1 · Get your log

In the Rotorflight Configurator, open the Blackbox tab and save your flight log to your computer. It will be a .bbl file — that's the raw recording, and Blackbox Lab reads it directly. (CSV exports work too.)

2 · Open it — read your Verdict

Click Open Blackbox Log on the Home screen. Blackbox Lab decodes the flight and gives you a plain-language Verdict: what looks healthy, what's worth watching, what needs attention — each with a button that jumps straight to the evidence.

3 · Follow the evidence

Every claim comes with a chart. Drag on any chart to zoom, double-click to reset. The Labs go deeper in the same order Rotorflight tuning does: vibration & filters first, then PIDs, then governor, then power.

4 · Change ONE thing, fly again

Blackbox Lab never touches your helicopter's settings — it recommends, you decide. Make one change at a time in the Rotorflight Configurator, fly, log, and open the new log here to see whether it helped. That loop is how tuning works.

5 · Compare — the loop closes

After the next flight, open Compare Flights: before vs after, and one sentence per topic tells you whether your change helped. Meanwhile the Health Record quietly tracks every analyzed flight per craft and warns you when vibration or droop trends the wrong way across sessions.

6 · Share when you need help

The Reports screen builds a single file with your verdict, findings and charts — ready to post on a forum or send to a friend when you want a second pair of eyes.

Words you'll meet (no jargon left behind)

Gyro — how fast the helicopter actually rotates, per axis. Setpoint — how fast you asked it to rotate. 1/rev — a vibration that happens once per main-rotor turn (usually balance). Headspeed — main rotor rpm. Droop — headspeed dipping under load. Saturation — the ESC at 100% with nothing left to give.
